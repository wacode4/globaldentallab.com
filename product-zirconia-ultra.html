Ultra Translucent Zirconia

An esthetic zirconia option for highly visible restorations that still need the reassurance of zirconia-family strength.

Use this product when the case sits in the smile zone, translucency matters, and the clinic wants a zirconia route rather than moving directly into lithium disilicate or layered alternatives.

Best for

Anterior crowns, visible implant restorations, and esthetic fixed cases.

Why clinics choose it

It gives a more appearance-led zirconia option without defaulting to a fully different ceramic family.

Workflow

Works best with digital scans, shade notes, prep photos, and clear esthetic direction.

Submission

Strong fit for cases where the doctor needs both esthetic positioning and zirconia familiarity.

Where This Product Fits Best

Ultra translucent zirconia usually enters the conversation when the case is visible, the doctor prefers a zirconia path, and esthetics still carry most of the decision.

1

Anterior Crowns

When translucency and visual integration matter more than a standard posterior zirconia approach.

2

Visible Implant Cases

For implant restorations in esthetic zones where the clinic still prefers a zirconia-family material decision.

3

Bridge Discussions

Useful when the case needs stronger esthetic positioning than monolithic zirconia but does not move into a fully different route.

DECISION CHECKPOINTS

What helps this zirconia workflow perform well

Prep clarity

Margin visibility and preparation intent matter more when esthetic expectations are higher.

Shade communication

Photos, provisional references, and shade notes help prevent generic-looking results in visible areas.

Material expectation

This is strongest when the clinic wants zirconia-family reassurance but a more esthetic presentation than monolithic zirconia.

Compared with monolithic zirconia

More esthetic positioning, less purely strength-led decision-making.

Compared with layered zirconia

Simpler route when the case needs translucency but not the same degree of layered finishing emphasis.

Compared with IPS e.max

Often chosen when the doctor still wants the familiarity and confidence of a zirconia pathway while staying highly esthetic.
