IPS e.max Lithium Disilicate

A lithium disilicate ceramic workflow for visible restorations that need translucency, shade control, and a more lifelike final presentation.

Use IPS e.max when the case sits in the smile zone, the clinic wants a glass-ceramic route, and esthetics carry more weight than a purely strength-first material decision.

Best for

Anterior crowns, veneers, inlays, onlays, and smile-focused restorative cases.

Why clinics choose it

Natural light behavior and refined shade presentation for appearance-led treatment plans.

Workflow

Performs best with scans, prep photos, shade notes, and clear esthetic direction.

Submission

Strong fit for doctors who want a premium ceramic route for visible single-unit or short-span cases.

Where This Product Fits Best

IPS e.max usually enters the conversation when the case is highly visible, the doctor wants a glass-ceramic route, and natural-looking esthetics are a priority.

1

Anterior Single Units

For visible crowns where translucency and shade integration matter more than maximum bulk strength.

2

Veneers And Minimal Coverage

Works well in cases that benefit from conservative preparation and a highly esthetic ceramic presentation.

3

Inlays And Onlays

Useful when the treatment plan favors bonded ceramic restorations with refined anatomy and color matching.

DECISION CHECKPOINTS

What helps this ceramic workflow perform well

Prep design

Material thickness, finish-line clarity, and reduction strategy affect how natural the final case can look.

Shade communication

Photos, stump shade references, and provisional feedback reduce guesswork in visible cases.

Material expectation

This route is strongest when esthetics lead the decision and the clinic is not defaulting to a zirconia-first workflow.

Compared with ultra translucent zirconia

Often chosen when the doctor wants a glass-ceramic presentation rather than a zirconia-family material path.

Compared with layered zirconia

A cleaner route for single units and conservative cases that do not need layered zirconia support.

Pairs naturally with veneers and inlays

This material family supports smile-design and bonded restorations where esthetic continuity matters across multiple units.
