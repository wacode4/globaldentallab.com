MATERIAL DECISION GUIDE

How clinics usually decide inside the ceramic category

If esthetics lead

Start with ultra translucent zirconia, IPS e.max, or veneers depending on restoration type and smile-zone demands.

If durability leads

Monolithic zirconia usually offers the simplest path when posterior strength and longer-term resilience are the priority.

If balance matters

Layered zirconia sits between brute strength and more nuanced esthetic finishing.

If structure preservation matters

Veneers, inlays, and onlays are stronger fits for conservative preparation strategies.

Lithium Disilicate Route

Well suited to appearance-led crowns, veneers, and smile-zone esthetic planning.

Zirconia Route

Useful where clinics want predictable zirconia-family choices across esthetic and strength-driven cases.

Shade and Finishing Discipline

Photos, shade references, provisional feedback, and prep clarity matter more in this category than generic material labels alone.
